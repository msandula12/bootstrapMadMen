Characters

Mad Men is loved for its accurate depiction of the look and feel of the 1950s-70s, but the show would be nothing without its rich cast of characters. From Don and the Draper clan to the folks at Sterling Cooper & Partners, and beyond. Here are some of the show's most memorable.


Don Draper

"People tell you who they are, but we ignore it because we want them to be who we want them to be."

Peggy Olson

“I don’t think anyone wants to be one of a hundred colors in a box."

Sally Draper

"When I think about forever I get upset. Like the Land of Lakes butter has that Indian girl, sitting holding a box, and it has a picture of her on it, holding a box, with a picture of her on it, holding a box. Have you ever noticed that?"

Roger Sterling

"What are the events in life? It’s like you see a door. The first time you come to it, you say, Oh, what’s on the other side of the door? Then you open a few doors. Then you say, I think I want to go over that bridge this time, I’m tired of doors. Finally you go through one of these things, and you come out the other side, and you realize, that’s all there are, doors, and windows and bridges and gates and they all open the same way and they all close behind you. Look, life is supposed to be a path, and you go along and these things happen to you, and they’re supposed to change you, change your direction. But turns out that’s not true. Turns out the experiences are nothing, they’re just some pennies you pick up off the floor, you stick in your pocket, and you’re just going in a straight line to you know where."

Bert Cooper

"I just wanted to say 'Happy Birthday!'"

Pete Campbell

"Not great, Bob!"

Betty Draper/Francis

"Only boring people are bored."

Joan Holloway/Harris

Michael Ginsberg

Cocktails

The popularity of Mad Men helped restore cocktail culture at bars and homes across America. Here are some of the drinks frequently featured on the show.


  • Old Fashioned
  • Manhattan
  • Vodka Martini
  • Gimlet

Don Draper's go-to.

  • 3 oz. whiskey (preferably bourbon)
  • Sugar cube
  • Splash of water
  • Angostura bitters
  • Maraschino cherry
  • Orange peel

Muddle a cube of sugar with a splash of water in a rocks glass. Pour in 3 oz. of bourbon whiskey. Add plenty of ice and stir. Garnish with a Maraschino cherry and an orange peel.

(If that's too complicated, you can always opt for Don's old standby: a neat pour of standard issue Canadian Club.)


When in Manhattan...

  • 3 oz. whiskey (we recommend rye)
  • 1 oz. sweet vermouth
  • Angostura bitters
  • Maraschino cherry

Pour 1 oz. sweet vermouth into a martini shaker with ice. Shake. Pour out excess vermouth. Add 3 oz. rye whiskey to shaker. Shake again. Strain into a rocks glass filled with fresh ice. Garnish with a Maraschino cherry.


Don't let Roger Sterling see the bottom of the glass.

  • 3 oz. vodka
  • 1 oz. dry vermouth
  • 3 stuffed olives on a toothpick

Pour 1 oz. dry vermouth into a martini shaker with ice. Shake. Pour out excess vermouth. Add 3 oz. vodka to shaker. Shake again. Strain into a chilled martini glass. Garnish with skewered olives.


Betty's favorite drink resembles herself: refreshing, with plenty of bite.

  • 3 oz. gin
  • 1 oz. lime juice
  • Wedge of lime

Pour 1 oz. lime juice and 3 oz. gin into a martini shaker with ice. Shake. Strain into a chilled martini glass. Garnish with a lime wedge.

Please drink responsibly!
